# CTFs
I absolutely love [CTF's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capture_the_flag_(cybersecurity)). Me and my team have won a lot of CTFs... but more than that almost every CTF I have participated in has taught me something new, stuff that I never knew even existed. I truly believe that these challenge authors live in a different plane of existencee. You can probably guess how excited I was when I had the oppurtunity to be one as well.

I had the amazing oppurtunity to organize two [CTF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capture_the_flag_(cybersecurity)) competitions this year, one for my college and another for the [DCG Coimbatore Chapter](https://dcg91422.org/) and oh boy was it stressful. 


Was it worth it? Absolutely. 

"It can't be that hard", I told myself. But then we had to deal with a myriad issues from hosting, challenge creation, physical infra and the biggest one of them all... people. Thankfully due to the amazing team I had we were able to conduct both CTFs successfully with little to no hiccup. (Both of them being different teams, but amazing teams nonetheless)

# Digging Deeper
My first priority is to stop procrastinating on [pwn.college](https://pwn.college/) and get my blue belt! This has been on my bucketlist for quite a while and solving one challenge at a time, getting closer to my goal one step at a time is really fun and rewarding. 

With all these AI models, I started losing my motivation to learn new things... For a period of time I also refrained from playing CTFs for the very same reason. Later I [this post by lake](https://lakectf.epfl.ch/no-llm.html) which restored some of my faith. I realised that I had lost sight of my original reason and love for playing CTFs. Now I avoid using LLMs in CTFs and I play for a very simple reason, to learn!
